# Lesson 0.1 — The Digital SAT Math Section: Know the Test Cold

Before studying a single math topic, you must know exactly what you are walking into. High scorers treat the test format as a known quantity.

## Format at a glance

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Platform | Bluebook app (laptop/tablet), digital only |
| Total math time | 70 minutes |
| Structure | 2 modules × 35 minutes × 22 questions = 44 questions |
| Time per question | ~95 seconds average |
| Adaptive? | Yes — Module 2 difficulty depends on Module 1 performance |
| Calculator | Allowed on **every** question; built-in Desmos graphing calculator provided |
| Question types | ~75% multiple choice (4 options), ~25% student-produced response (type in your answer) |
| Scoring | 200–800, no penalty for wrong answers — **never leave a blank** |
| Reference sheet | Geometry formulas provided on-screen (see [formula sheet](../07-reference/formula-sheet.md)) |

## The adaptive structure — what it means for you

- **Module 1** is a mix of easy, medium, and hard questions.
- If you do well on Module 1, **Module 2 is the harder version** — and only the harder version gives access to top scores.
- To score 750+, you must reach the hard Module 2 **and** perform well on it. There is no path to a high score through the easy Module 2.
- Practical consequence: **Module 1 accuracy is sacred.** A sloppy Module 1 caps your score no matter how brilliant you are in Module 2.

A small number of questions on the test are unscored experimental items, but you can't identify them — treat every question as real.

## The four content domains

| Domain | Weight | Questions | Course Unit |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Algebra | ~35% | 13–15 | [Unit 1](../01-algebra/) |
| Advanced Math | ~35% | 13–15 | [Unit 2](../02-advanced-math/) |
| Problem-Solving & Data Analysis | ~15% | 5–7 | [Unit 3](../03-problem-solving-data-analysis/) |
| Geometry & Trigonometry | ~15% | 5–7 | [Unit 4](../04-geometry-trigonometry/) |

### What each domain contains

**Algebra** — linear equations in one and two variables, systems of linear equations, linear inequalities, linear functions, interpreting linear models.

**Advanced Math** — quadratics, polynomials, exponents and radicals, exponential functions, rational expressions, absolute value, function notation and transformations, nonlinear systems, equivalent expressions.

**Problem-Solving & Data Analysis** — ratios, rates, proportions, percentages, unit conversion, one-variable statistics (mean/median/mode/range/standard deviation), two-variable data (scatterplots, lines of best fit), probability, two-way tables, sampling and margin of error, evaluating statistical claims.

**Geometry & Trigonometry** — angles and parallel lines, triangles (congruence, similarity), right-triangle trigonometry, the unit circle basics, circles (equations, arcs, sectors), area, surface area, volume.

**That list is exhaustive.** Nothing outside it appears on the SAT. No calculus, no logarithms, no matrices, no proofs, no sequences/series formulas.

## Student-Produced Responses (SPR / "fill-ins")

About 5–6 questions per module have no answer choices — you type the answer.

Rules:
- Up to 5 characters for positive answers, 6 for negative (the `-` counts as a character).
- Fractions (`7/2`) and decimals (`3.5`) both accepted. **Don't round unless required** — if the answer is 2/3, enter `2/3` or `.6666`/`.6667` (fill all character spaces), never `0.66` or `0.67`... `.67` is wrong.
- No mixed numbers: 2½ must be `5/2` or `2.5`, never `21/2`.
- If multiple correct answers exist, enter any one of them.

Full drill in [Lesson 5.4](../05-strategies/04-student-produced-responses.md).

## Scoring reality check

Approximate (varies slightly by test form):

| Raw performance | Scaled score |
|-----------------|--------------|
| Perfect (44/44) | 800 |
| 1 miss | 770–800 |
| 2–3 misses | 740–770 |
| 4–5 misses | 700–740 |
| 7–8 misses | 650–700 |

The takeaway: **at the 750+ level, every single question matters.** The difference between 730 and 790 is often two careless errors, not two hard questions.

## Your on-screen tools in Bluebook

- **Desmos graphing calculator** (embedded) — the single most powerful tool on the test. [Lesson 0.6](06-desmos-mastery.md) is mandatory.
- **Mark for Review** — flag a question and return to it within the module. You cannot return to Module 1 once Module 2 starts.
- **Option eliminator** — cross out answer choices you've ruled out. Use it; it reduces careless errors.
- **Reference sheet** — geometry formulas. Know them anyway; opening the sheet costs time.
- **Countdown timer** — can be hidden. Recommended: visible, with the [pacing checkpoints from Lesson 5.5](../05-strategies/05-time-management.md).
