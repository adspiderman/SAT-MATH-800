# Unit 3 Quiz — Problem-Solving & Data Analysis

**12 questions, 18 minutes, Desmos allowed.** Questions 11–12 are student-produced response.

---

**1.** The ratio of cats to dogs at a shelter is 4:7. If there are 44 animals total (all cats or dogs), how many dogs are there?
A) 16  B) 24  C) 28  D) 33

**2.** After a 30% discount, a coat costs $84. What was the original price?
A) $109.20  B) $114  C) $120  D) $126

**3.** A cyclist rides 24 miles in 90 minutes. What is her speed in miles per hour?
A) 12  B) 16  C) 18  D) 24

**4.** The mean of 6 numbers is 15. Five of them are 12, 14, 15, 16, and 18. What is the sixth?
A) 15  B) 14  C) 13  D) 12

**5.** Data set A: {10, 10, 11, 11}. Data set B: {5, 8, 13, 16}. Both have the same mean. Which statement is true?
A) A has the larger standard deviation
B) B has the larger standard deviation
C) The standard deviations are equal
D) It cannot be determined

**6.** A best-fit line for a scatterplot is ŷ = 5 + 2x. A data point is at (6, 20). The actual y-value exceeds the predicted by
A) 1  B) 2  C) 3  D) 17

**7.** Use the table:

|  | Passed | Failed | Total |
|--|--------|--------|-------|
| Morning class | 32 | 8 | 40 |
| Evening class | 42 | 18 | 60 |
| Total | 74 | 26 | 100 |

Given that a randomly chosen student passed, what is the probability they were in the morning class?
A) 32/100  B) 32/74  C) 32/40  D) 74/100

**8.** A random sample of 200 households in Town X finds a mean of 2.6 cars with margin of error 0.3. Which is the best conclusion?
A) Every household in Town X owns 2.3–2.9 cars
B) The mean number of cars per household in Town X is plausibly between 2.3 and 2.9
C) The mean number of cars in neighboring towns is between 2.3 and 2.9
D) Exactly 2.6 cars per household is the true mean

**9.** A store raises a price 25%, then offers "20% off" the new price. The final price is what percent of the original?
A) 95%  B) 100%  C) 105%  D) 110%

**10.** A researcher randomly assigns randomly selected plants from a greenhouse to fertilizer A or B. A-fertilized plants grow taller. The strongest valid conclusion is:
A) Fertilizer A causes taller growth in this greenhouse's plants
B) Fertilizer A causes taller growth in all plants everywhere
C) There is an association but no causal link
D) No conclusion is possible

**11.** *(SPR)* A car travels 150 miles using 6 gallons. At the same rate, how many gallons does a 400-mile trip use?

**12.** *(SPR)* Set S = {3, 7, 8, 12, x}. If the median of S is 8 and the mean is 9, what is x?

---

## Answer Key

| # | Answer | Why |
|---|--------|-----|
| 1 | C | 11 parts of 4 each → dogs = 7 × 4 = 28 |
| 2 | C | 84/0.70 = 120 |
| 3 | B | 24 mi / 1.5 h |
| 4 | A | total 90 − 75 = 15 |
| 5 | B | same center, more spread |
| 6 | C | predicted 17; 20 − 17 = 3 |
| 7 | B | denominator = all who passed (74) |
| 8 | B | margin of error = plausible range for the population mean, Town X only |
| 9 | B | 1.25 × 0.80 = 1.00 |
| 10 | A | random assignment → causation; random selection from the greenhouse → that population |
| 11 | 16 | 400/25 (25 mpg) |
| 12 | 15 | mean 9 → sum 45 → x = 15; check median: {3,7,8,12,15} → 8 ✓ |
