# Lesson 3.1 — Ratios, Rates & Proportions

## Concepts

### Ratios — parts of a whole

A ratio 3:5 means the whole splits into 3k and 5k for some k.

**Worked example 1.** Two numbers are in ratio 3:5 and sum to 40.
- 3k + 5k = 8k = 40 → k = 5 → numbers are 15 and 25.

**Chaining ratios.** If a:b = 2:3 and b:c = 4:5, scale so b matches: a:b = 8:12, b:c = 12:15 → **a:c = 8:15**.

### Proportions — equal ratios

Set up with labels aligned (same units on tops, same on bottoms), then cross-multiply.

**Worked example 2.** A map uses 1 inch : 40 miles. How far is 3.5 inches?
- 1/40 = 3.5/x → x = **140 miles**.

**Worked example 3.** x/12 = 15/20 → 20x = 180 → **x = 9**.

### Rates — a ratio with different units

speed = distance/time; unit price = cost/quantity; any "per" is a division.
- 210 miles in 3.5 hours → 60 mph.
- $2.40 per dozen eggs → $0.20 per egg.

**Rate problems are unit problems**: write the units into your fractions and cancel them like factors ([Lesson 3.3](03-units-and-conversion.md)).

### Scaling recipes / mixtures

2 cups of flour make 3 dozen cookies. For 5 dozen: flour = 2 × (5/3) = **10/3 cups**. Multiply by the scale factor of the *other* quantity.

## Desmos angle

Proportions are one-liners: type `1/40 = 3.5/x` — Desmos solves it (or graph `y = x/12` and `y = 15/20`... simpler to just arithmetic it). Best use here: fast fraction arithmetic without decimal-rounding errors — keep numbers as fractions.

## Traps

1. **Part:part vs part:whole.** Boys:girls = 2:3 means boys are 2/5 of the class, not 2/3. The SAT's favorite ratio trap.
2. **Order.** "the ratio of A to B" = A/B. Reversing it inverts the answer, and the inverted answer is always among the choices.
3. Ratios must compare same units — convert feet/inches, hours/minutes first.
4. A ratio 3:5 doesn't mean the actual numbers are 3 and 5 — they're 3k and 5k.

## Practice Set

1. Two numbers are in ratio 3:5 and sum to 40. What is the larger?
2. A recipe uses 2 cups of flour for 3 dozen cookies. How much flour for 5 dozen?
3. A map scale is 1 inch : 40 miles. Two cities are 3.5 inches apart on the map. Actual distance?
4. A car travels 210 miles in 3.5 hours. What is its average speed?
5. Solve: x/12 = 15/20
6. If a:b = 2:3 and b:c = 4:5, what is a:c?
7. A class of 45 students has 18 boys. What is the ratio of boys to girls?
8. Eggs cost $2.40 per dozen. What is the cost of 30 eggs?
9. Paint is mixed with red and white in ratio 3:2. How many quarts of red are needed for 30 quarts of mixture?
10. A printer prints 45 pages in 90 seconds. At this rate, how many pages in 4 minutes?

### Answers

1. **25** — parts 15 and 25.
2. **10/3 cups** (≈3.33).
3. **140 miles**.
4. **60 mph**.
5. **x = 9**.
6. **8:15** — scale b to 12.
7. **2:3** — 18 boys, 27 girls.
8. **$6.00** — $0.20 each × 30.
9. **18** — red is 3/5 of the total.
10. **120** — 0.5 pages/sec × 240 sec.
