# Lesson 3.2 — Percentages

## Concepts

### The one identity

```
part = percent × whole        (percent as a decimal)
```

All three variants: What is 30% of 150? (45). 30 is what % of 150? (20%). 45 is 30% of what? (150).

### Percent change

```
% change = (new − old)/old × 100
```

Population 250 → 290: (40/250) = 16% increase. **The denominator is always the ORIGINAL.**

### The multiplier method (use this for everything)

| Change | Multiplier |
|--------|-----------|
| increase 25% | × 1.25 |
| decrease 20% | × 0.80 |
| increase 8% then 10% | × 1.08 × 1.10 |

**Worked example 1.** $80 jacket, price increased 25% → 80 × 1.25 = **$100**.

**Worked example 2 (successive changes).** A price drops 20%, then rises 20%.
- × 0.8 × 1.2 = × 0.96 → **4% net decrease**. Not back to the start! Percent changes never cancel because they act on different bases.

**Worked example 3 (reverse percent).** After a 15% discount, a jacket costs $51. Original?
- 0.85x = 51 → x = **$60**. (NOT 51 × 1.15 = 58.65 — that's the trap.)

### Percent in tables and mixtures

"What percent of the juniors chose option A" → part = juniors choosing A, whole = **all juniors** (not everyone). Locating the right whole is the entire skill.

## Desmos angle

Straight arithmetic — use the calculator freely: `51/0.85`, `80*1.25`. For reverse-percent MC questions, backsolve: multiply each choice by the given multiplier and see which lands on the stated price.

## Traps

1. **Reverse percent:** undo a −15% with ÷0.85, never ×1.15.
2. **Percent change denominator = original value.** From 40 to 50 is +25%; from 50 to 40 is −20%. Direction matters.
3. **Successive percents multiply**, never add: +50% then +50% is ×2.25 (+125%), not +100%.
4. **Percent vs percentage points:** going from 10% to 15% is +5 percentage points but a 50% increase.
5. "Increased BY 200%" = ×3. "Increased TO 200%" = ×2.

## Practice Set

1. 30 is what percent of 150?
2. What is 12% of 250?
3. 45 is 30% of what number?
4. An $80 jacket's price increases 25%. New price?
5. After a 15% discount, a jacket costs $51. What was the original price?
6. A price drops 20%, then rises 20%. What is the net percent change?
7. A town's population grew from 250 to 290. What was the percent increase?
8. An item costs $25 plus 8% sales tax. Total cost?
9. In a survey, 40% of 350 respondents said yes. How many said yes?
10. A stock rises 10% one year and falls 10% the next. It ends at $198. What was its starting price?

### Answers

1. **20%**.
2. **30**.
3. **150**.
4. **$100**.
5. **$60** — 51/0.85.
6. **−4%** — ×0.96.
7. **16%** — 40/250.
8. **$27.00** — 25 × 1.08.
9. **140**.
10. **$200** — x(1.10)(0.90) = 0.99x = 198 → x = 200.
