# Lesson 5.5 — Time Management

35 minutes, 22 questions = **95 seconds per question**. But the right plan is not uniform — it's front-loaded easy, back-loaded hard.

## The pacing map

Digital SAT modules run *roughly* easy → hard (not strictly, but the trend is real).

| Segment | Questions | Budget | Pace |
|---------|-----------|--------|------|
| 1 | Q1–8 (mostly easy) | 8 min | ~60 s each |
| 2 | Q9–16 (medium) | 12 min | ~90 s each |
| 3 | Q17–22 (hard) | 12 min | ~2 min each |
| Reserve | marked questions + blank check | 3 min | — |

## The three checkpoints (memorize)

Check the clock exactly three times:
- **After Q8: ~27 minutes left.** Behind? Speed up on mediums, lean on Desmos.
- **After Q16: ~15 minutes left.** Behind? Triage the remaining six — do the four you like, guess-and-mark the two you don't.
- **5 minutes left: all questions answered?** If not, fill every blank NOW, then return to marked ones.

Checking the timer constantly costs focus; never checking costs modules. Three looks is the balance.

## The 100-second rule

If you've worked ~100 seconds on a question and don't have a clear path: **stop**, eliminate what you can, pick the best remaining choice, mark for review, move on. The math: rescuing one brutal question at the cost of rushing three easy ones is a net loss of 2 questions. At 750+, the enemy isn't the hard question — it's the careless miss it *causes downstream*.

## Speed comes from method choice, not hurrying

The fast solver isn't moving their pencil faster — they chose a 30-second method:
- Systems → Desmos intersection, not elimination drills.
- "Sum of solutions" → −b/a, not solving.
- Variable answer choices → plug in numbers.
- Concrete answer choices, convoluted setup → backsolve from B/C.
- Equivalent expressions → Desmos graph-match.

If your practice-test review shows a question you got right in 4 minutes, that's still a *time miss* — find the 60-second method in review and log it.

## Building speed in practice (Phase 3)

1. **Timed sections only after untimed accuracy is ≥95%** on that unit ([Lesson 0.2](../00-orientation/02-how-to-use-this-course.md)).
2. Practice with the same three-checkpoint rhythm — make it automatic before test day.
3. Track your Q17–22 performance separately: hard-question accuracy under time pressure is the 800 gate.
4. When reviewing, sort misses into "didn't know" vs "knew but rushed" — a rushed miss is a *pacing* bug, fixed by trusting the checkpoints, not by more content study.

## If you finish early

Never submit early. In order:
1. Re-check marked questions with a *different method* (Desmos if you did algebra, plug-in if you computed).
2. Re-read the final sentence of Q17–22 — wrong-target errors hide there.
3. Verify SPR entries character by character.
