# Lesson 5.3 — The Trap Catalog

The SAT writes wrong answer choices *on purpose* — each distractor is a specific predictable error. Learn the catalog and you'll recognize the bait mid-problem. **At the 750+ level, this lesson is worth more than any content lesson.**

## The Big Ten

### 1. The Wrong-Target Trap (most points lost, test-wide)
You solve for x; the question wanted x + 2, 2x, y, the larger solution, the *number of* solutions. The value of x is sitting right there in the choices.
**Defense:** re-read the final sentence *after* solving. Circle the target on scratch paper first.

### 2. The Flip Trap (inequalities)
Dividing by a negative without flipping. The unflipped answer is always choice A or B.
**Defense:** any negative division = write "FLIP" on scratch.

### 3. The Extraneous Root Trap (radicals & rationals)
Squaring/clearing denominators creates fake solutions; the fake one is a choice.
**Defense:** test every candidate in the ORIGINAL equation ([2.1](../02-advanced-math/01-exponents-and-radicals.md), [2.6](../02-advanced-math/06-rational-expressions-and-equations.md)).

### 4. The Sign-Flip Vertex/Center Trap
Vertex of (x + 3)² − 4 is (−3, −4), not (3, 4) — and (3, 4) *will* be a choice. Same for circle centers.
**Defense:** "the form eats the sign" — read h and k by matching the template exactly.

### 5. The Percent Base Trap
Reverse percent solved forward (×1.15 instead of ÷0.85); successive percents added instead of multiplied; percent change divided by the new value.
**Defense:** multiplier method always; denominator = original ([3.2](../03-problem-solving-data-analysis/02-percentages.md)).

### 6. The Denominator Trap (conditional probability)
Using the grand total instead of the "given" group's total.
**Defense:** the phrase after "given that"/"of those who" IS the denominator ([3.6](../03-problem-solving-data-analysis/06-probability-two-way-tables.md)).

### 7. The Unit Trap
Rate per minute, question in hours; feet vs inches; diameter given, radius needed. Squared conversions (1 ft² = 144 in²).
**Defense:** underline every unit; convert *before* computing ([3.3](../03-problem-solving-data-analysis/03-units-and-conversion.md)).

### 8. The Middle-Term Trap
(x + 3)² = x² + 9 (missing 6x); a² − b² "factored" as (a − b)². Both appear as wrong equivalents.
**Defense:** FOIL discipline — or Desmos graph-match both expressions.

### 9. The Ratio Direction Trap
"Ratio of A to B" answered as B/A; part:part treated as part:whole; similar-triangle ratios inverted.
**Defense:** write the ratio with labels before numbers: A/B = ...

### 10. The "Looks Solved" Trap (special cases)
px + q = rx + s questions where the answer is "no solution" or "infinitely many" — students grind toward a number that doesn't exist. Also |x| = negative, discriminant < 0.
**Defense:** when coefficients can match, check the special cases *first*.

## Trap-proofing routine (build the habit in practice)

Before submitting any answer, a 3-second checklist:
1. **Target** — is this the quantity asked for?
2. **Units** — does the answer's unit match the question's?
3. **Sanity** — is the magnitude plausible? (A 250-mph cyclist, a −3-foot side, a probability of 1.4 → recheck.)

During review of practice tests, label every miss with its trap number. Two misses on the same trap = that trap goes on a sticky note for your next test.

## Drill

Each item below contains one planted trap. Name it, then solve correctly.

1. If 3x + 5 = 20, what is 6x + 5?
2. Solve −5x + 3 > 18.
3. The price fell 40%, then rose 50%. Net change?
4. Vertex of y = (x − 2)² + 7?
5. √(3x + 4) = x → candidates x = 4, x = −1. Answer?
6. Of the 60 seniors, 24 play a sport. Of the 40 juniors, 20 play. What is P(senior | plays a sport)?
7. A circle has diameter 18. Area?
8. Water flows at 4 gal/min. How many gallons in 2 hours?
9. In similar triangles, small:big = 2:3 and the big triangle's side is 12. The corresponding small side?
10. Solve 2(3x + 1) = 6x + 2.

### Answers

1. **35** (Trap 1) — 3x = 15 → 6x = 30 → 35. Not x = 5.
2. **x < −3** (Trap 2) — flip.
3. **−10%** (Trap 5) — 0.6 × 1.5 = 0.9.
4. **(2, 7)** (Trap 4) — signs: h = +2.
5. **x = 4 only** (Trap 3) — x = −1 fails the original (√1 = 1 ≠ −1).
6. **24/44 = 6/11** (Trap 6) — denominator is all athletes (44), not all students.
7. **81π** (Trap 7) — r = 9.
8. **480** (Trap 7) — 2 h = 120 min.
9. **8** (Trap 9) — 12 × 2/3.
10. **Infinitely many solutions** (Trap 10) — both sides identical.
