# Lesson 5.4 — Student-Produced Responses (Fill-Ins)

About 11 of the 44 math questions have no answer choices. They're not harder — but they remove two safety nets (no backsolving, no "my answer isn't listed" alarm), so verification matters more.

## The entry rules (know these cold)

1. **Length:** up to 5 characters for positive answers, up to 6 for negative (the `-` sign counts).
2. **Fractions are fine:** `7/2` is a valid entry. No mixed numbers — `21/2` reads as twenty-one halves, so enter 2½ as `5/2` or `2.5`.
3. **Decimals:** either round to fit or (better) truncate/fill all spaces. For 2/3: `2/3`, `.6666`, or `.6667` are accepted; `0.66` or `.66` are **wrong**.
4. **Don't enter symbols:** no %, $, π, √, commas, or units. If the answer is 40%, enter `40` (the question will say "in percent") — if it asks for the *decimal* probability, `.4`.
5. **Multiple correct answers:** enter any ONE of them.
6. **Negative answers exist**; there is no "can't be negative" rule (but many contexts make negatives impossible — sanity check).

## Strategy adjustments for SPR

- **Prefer fractions over decimals** — exact, shorter, no rounding-rule risk. If your work produces 5/12, type `5/12`; converting to .4166... invites error.
- **Desmos gives decimals; convert carefully.** If Desmos shows 0.41666..., recognize 5/12 (type the decimal into Desmos as a fraction check: `5/12` shows the same value) — or just enter `.4166`/`.4167` correctly.
- **Verification is mandatory** at the 800 level: plug your answer back into the original conditions. SPR has no wrong-answer choices to warn you.
- "What is one possible value..." → find the easiest one, not the general solution. If 3 < x < 4 works, enter `3.5`.
- Answers are typically "nice-ish": integers, simple fractions, one-decimal values. Getting 17.8342 should trigger a re-read (unless the question says "round to...").

## Worked micro-examples

| Situation | Enter |
|-----------|-------|
| Answer is 2/3 | `2/3` (or `.6666` / `.6667`) |
| Answer is 3½ | `7/2` or `3.5` |
| Answer is −1/4 | `-1/4` or `-.25` |
| Answer is $12.50, question asks dollars | `12.5` |
| Answer is 66⅔%, question asks percent | `66.6` / `66.7` / `200/3` |
| Solutions are −3 and 5, "one possible value" | either `-3` or `5` |

## Drill (write your exact keypad entry)

1. x² = 5. One possible value of x?
2. The probability is 3/8. Entry?
3. Solve 4x − 6 = 5 and enter x.
4. A price of $1,240.50 — question asks "in dollars."
5. The answer is the repeating decimal 0.833...; give two acceptable entries.
6. Mean of 11, 14, 22 — entry?
7. Solve: 6/x = 8/3.
8. An angle measures 5π/6 radians; the question asks for the measure in degrees.
9. If 2 < w < 3, one possible value of 2w?
10. x = −0.375 exactly. Give two acceptable entries.

### Answers

1. `2.236` or `-2.23` — √5 ≈ 2.2360; a 5-character positive entry `2.236` is accepted (fills the spaces). Either sign works since both square to 5.
2. `3/8` (or `.375`)
3. `11/4` (or `2.75`) — 4x = 11.
4. `1240.5` won't fit in 5 characters — and that's the lesson: **real SAT answers always fit the entry box.** If your value doesn't fit, your value is wrong; re-solve.
5. `5/6` or `.8333` — truncating to fill all spaces is accepted; `0.83` or `.83` is not. The fraction is safest.
6. `47/3` — the mean is 47/3 = 15.666..., and `15.66`/`15.67` uses 5 characters where the repeating decimal really wants more precision than fits comfortably; SAT scoring accepts `15.66` (truncated, filled) and `15.67` (rounded, filled), but the fraction has zero risk. **When a decimal repeats, enter the fraction.**
7. `9/4` (or `2.25`) — 8x = 18.
8. `150` — 5π/6 × 180/π.
9. Any value strictly between 4 and 6, e.g. `5`.
10. `-.375` or `-3/8` — six characters are allowed for negatives.
