# Lesson 5.6 — The Error Log System

**This is the single highest-leverage practice in the course.** Practice tests locate your errors; the log converts them into points. Students who skip this plateau; students who run it climb ~50–100 points in weeks.

## The log format

Keep a spreadsheet (or copy this table into one). One row per miss — from lessons, quizzes, practice tests, official tests, everything.

| Date | Source | Topic (lesson #) | The problem (short) | My answer | Correct | **Error type** | **Root cause (one sentence)** | Re-do due | Re-done ✓ |
|------|--------|------------------|---------------------|-----------|---------|----------------|-------------------------------|-----------|-----------|
| 7/2 | PT1 M2 Q18 | 2.9 nonlinear | tangent line k value | 4 | −4 | Concept | Didn't connect "one solution" to discriminant = 0 | 7/5 | |
| 7/2 | PT1 M1 Q6 | 1.4 inequality | −3x > 12 | x > −4 | x < −4 | Careless | Forgot the flip | 7/5 | |

## The four error types (be honest — the fix differs)

1. **Concept** — didn't know the math. → Re-read the lesson, re-do its practice set.
2. **Misread** — knew the math, answered the wrong question / missed a condition. → Trap catalog ([5.3](03-common-traps.md)); build the final-sentence re-read habit.
3. **Careless** — knew it, executed wrong (sign, arithmetic, dropped term). → Slow down THAT step type; add a verification step (plug back in / Desmos).
4. **Time** — rushed or abandoned. → Pacing bug; revisit [5.5](05-time-management.md); usually means an earlier question stole your time.

## The re-do protocol (where the points come from)

- **3 days after logging:** re-solve the problem cold (cover the solution). Full success = check the box. Failure = re-study, new due date 3 more days out.
- **Weekly:** skim the Root Cause column. Patterns jump out — "forgot the flip" three times is not three errors; it's ONE habit to fix.
- **Before each practice test:** re-do the last 10 unchecked problems.
- **Final week before the real test:** your entire review is the error log + formula sheet. Nothing predicts your mistakes better than your mistakes.

## Why root cause must be one sentence

Writing "I need to be more careful" is useless. Writing "I distributed −3 into (x − 4) and got −3x − 12" names the exact synapse to fix. If you can't state the root cause in one concrete sentence, you haven't understood the miss yet — that's the signal to re-study.

## Milestones

- 25 logged errors → you know your top-2 weakness categories with confidence.
- 50 logged errors with re-dos done → careless rate typically halves.
- The 800 signal: your last two practice tests produce ≤2 new log rows each.
