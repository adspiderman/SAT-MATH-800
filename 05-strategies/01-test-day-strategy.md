# Lesson 5.1 — Test-Day Strategy

## The week before

- **Taper, don't cram.** Last full practice test no later than 3–4 days out. Final 2 days: error-log review, formula sheet skim, Desmos drill — light work only.
- Verify Bluebook runs on your device; charge it; know your test center rules (or home setup).
- Sleep schedule aligned with test time for at least 3 days prior.

## The morning

- Wake 3+ hours before. Eat real food. Arrive early.
- Bring: admission ticket, ID, charged device + charger, pencils and eraser for scratch work, water/snack for the break, watch-free wrist (Bluebook has the timer).
- Warm up your brain: 3–4 easy problems from your error log *before leaving home* — you want the first real question to be your tenth of the day, not your first.

## During the module — the operating system

1. **One pass, mark and move.** Answer everything you can within ~100 seconds each. Anything longer: pick your best elimination guess, **Mark for Review**, move on. Return in the end-of-module time.
2. **Never leave blanks** — no wrong-answer penalty. Even on marked questions, always have an answer selected before moving on.
3. **Use the option eliminator** on every question where you rule anything out. It offloads memory and prevents re-reading traps.
4. **Read the final sentence of the question twice.** Solve for what is *asked* (x+1, not x; the y-coordinate, not x; the count, not the probability). This one habit is worth 20+ points.
5. **Scratch paper discipline:** number your work by question. When you return to a marked question, your prior thinking is waiting.
6. Between modules: the Module 1 → 2 break in mindset matters. Module 1 done = gone. Never re-litigate it mentally during Module 2.

## The two-pass model in numbers

35 minutes, 22 questions:
- Pass 1 (~28 min): all 22 questions, marking the 2–4 brutal ones with a guess placed.
- Pass 2 (~7 min): return to marked questions with fresh eyes and Desmos.
- Final 30 seconds: confirm no blanks.

Full pacing detail: [Lesson 5.5](05-time-management.md).

## Mindset rules

- **A hard question is worth the same as an easy one.** Skipping a brutal #9 to bank three easy ones later is winning, not weakness.
- Hitting hard questions in Module 2 is **good news** — it means you earned the hard module, where the top scores live.
- One careless miss costs the same as one impossible question. The 800 mindset treats *verification* (plug your answer back in, Desmos check) as part of solving, not an optional extra.
- If panic rises: 5-second reset — exhale slowly, read the next question's last sentence, start writing. Motion kills anxiety.

## What NOT to do

- Don't change answers on a "feeling" during review — only change with a concrete reason (found an error, misread spotted).
- Don't time-audit every question ("am I behind??") — use the three checkpoints from [Lesson 5.5](05-time-management.md) only.
- Don't do full algebra when Desmos or backsolving ([Lesson 5.2](02-plugging-in-and-backsolving.md)) is faster — but don't force Desmos on symbolic questions either. The skill is the *choice*.
