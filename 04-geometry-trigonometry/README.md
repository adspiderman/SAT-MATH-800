# Unit 4 — Geometry & Trigonometry (~15% of the test, 5–7 questions)

Angles, triangles, right-triangle trig, circles, and solid geometry. The Digital SAT gives you a **formula reference sheet** on every question, so this unit is less about memorization and more about *recognizing which relationship applies* — usually similarity, the Pythagorean theorem, or a formula-sheet plug-in.

## The big ideas

1. **Similar triangles = equal ratios.** Half the hard geometry questions are hidden similarity.
2. **Right triangle spotted → Pythagorean theorem or trig.** Memorize the triples (3-4-5, 5-12-13, and multiples) and the two special triangles.
3. **sin θ = cos(90° − θ)** — the complementary-angle identity is a guaranteed SAT favorite.
4. **Circle equations are completing-the-square problems** in disguise.
5. **Scaling:** lengths ×k ⇒ areas ×k² ⇒ volumes ×k³.

## The reference sheet gives you (know it anyway — it's faster)

Area of circle/rectangle/triangle; Pythagorean theorem; special right triangles; circumference; volumes of box, cylinder, sphere, cone, pyramid; "the number of degrees in a circle is 360; the number of radians is 2π; the sum of angles in a triangle is 180."
