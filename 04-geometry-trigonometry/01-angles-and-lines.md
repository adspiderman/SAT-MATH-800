# Lesson 4.1 — Angles & Lines

## Concepts

### Angle basics

- Angles on a straight line sum to **180°** (linear pair / supplementary).
- Angles around a point sum to **360°**.
- **Vertical angles** (across an X) are **equal**.
- Complementary = sum to 90°; supplementary = sum to 180°.

### Parallel lines cut by a transversal — only two cases

When a line crosses two parallel lines, every angle formed is one of just two values, and the two values sum to 180°.

- Corresponding, alternate interior, alternate exterior angles → **equal**.
- Same-side (co-interior) angles → **supplementary**.

**Shortcut that never fails:** label one angle x; every other angle is x or 180° − x. Acute-looking = the small one, obtuse-looking = the big one (diagrams are drawn roughly to scale on the SAT unless noted).

**Worked example.** Lines ℓ ∥ m. A transversal makes a 65° angle with ℓ. A second transversal makes a 40° angle with m, and the two transversals meet between the lines at angle y. What is y?
- Draw a third parallel line through the vertex: y splits into alternate-interior partners of 65° and 40° → y = **105°**. (The "draw an auxiliary parallel line" move solves every zig-zag angle problem.)

### Polygon angle sums

- Triangle: 180°. Quadrilateral: 360°.
- n-gon: **(n − 2) × 180°**.
- Regular n-gon interior angle: (n − 2)·180/n. Exterior angles of any polygon sum to **360°**.

## Traps

1. "Looks equal" isn't a reason — cite vertical / corresponding / alternate. But DO use the diagram's scale to sanity-check your answer.
2. Same-side interior angles are supplementary, **not equal** — the one relationship people misremember.
3. The (n − 2)·180 formula counts **interior** angles. Exterior sum is always 360, any n.
4. Multi-transversal problems: auxiliary parallel line through the bend point.

## Practice Set

1. Two lines intersect; one angle formed is 38°. What are the other three?
2. ℓ ∥ m with a transversal; one angle is 112°. What is the measure of the acute angles formed?
3. Angles of measure x, 2x, and 105° lie on a straight line. Find x.
4. What is the sum of interior angles of a hexagon?
5. Each interior angle of a regular polygon measures 144°. How many sides?
6. In the zig-zag worked example above, redo it with transversal angles 50° and 30°. What is y?
7. Two complementary angles have measures 3x and 2x − 10. Find x.
8. ℓ ∥ m. A transversal creates same-side interior angles of measure 3x + 10 and 2x − 5. Find x.
9. An exterior angle of a regular polygon is 24°. How many sides?
10. Three lines meet at one point, forming six angles. Alternate angles around the point are 70°, x, and 50°, with the pattern repeating. What is x?

### Answers

1. **38°, 142°, 142°** — vertical pair equal; neighbors supplementary.
2. **68°** — 180 − 112.
3. **x = 25** — 3x + 105 = 180.
4. **720°** — (6 − 2)·180.
5. **10** — exterior angle 36° → 360/36.
6. **80°** — 50 + 30.
7. **x = 20** — 5x − 10 = 90.
8. **x = 35** — supplementary: 5x + 5 = 180.
9. **15** — 360/24.
10. **60°** — angles around a point on one side of a line: 70 + x + 50 = 180.
